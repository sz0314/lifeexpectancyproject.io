Background

Life expectancy at birth indicates the number of years a newborn infant would live if prevailing patterns of mortality at the time of its birth were to stay the same throughout its life. It is a key metric for assessing population health. Life expectancy has burgeoned since the advent of industrialization in the early 1900s and the world average has now more than doubled to 70 years. Yet, we still see inequality in life expectancy across and within countries.

In the context of life expectancy, a right-skewed distribution (left bottom) means that when you look at the entire population of a region or country, most people have relatively shorter lifespans, but there is a smaller group of individuals who have significantly longer lifespans. This smaller group of people with longer lifespans pulls the average (or mean) life expectancy to the right, creating a rightward skewness in the distribution.

Chart 3: Correlation between Economic Development and Life Expectancy

Description

Based on Chart 3, life expectancy is strongly positively correlated with sanitation levels and strongly negatively correlated with undernourishment. This observation aligns with what we know about the impact of sanitation and nutrition on life expectancy.

A strong positive correlation indicates that as sanitation levels improve, life expectancy tends to increase.

A strong negative correlation implies that as undernourishment decreases (i.e., people have better access to sufficient and nutritious food), life expectancy tends to rise.

Chart 4: Sanitation and Life Expectancy

Access to clean and safe sanitation facilities has a significant impact on the overall health and life expectancy of populations. In many regions with inadequate sanitation, life expectancy remains lower due to the increased risk of diseases.

However, with the improvement of sanitation Levels, life expectancy does not increase linearly. This implies that there are other factors constraining life expectancy. Similarly, as the prevalence of undernourishment increases, the decrease in life expectancy is also non-linear. Some countries exhibit steeper declines, indicating a high vulnerability.
